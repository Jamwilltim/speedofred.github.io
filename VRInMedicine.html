The impact of virtual and augmented reality in medicine.

Medicine is a vast field, with a new innovations being made every day. One of the more promising features includes the use of virtual and augmented reality (VR/AR). There are many applications for these technologies including

  • Improving the understanding of patients
  • Training
  • Theraputic techniques
  • Physical therapy

Improving the understandings of patients @TheGeorgeWashingtonUniversityHospital

The link to the article can be found here. In summary the hospital now uses VR to aid with "neurosurgery" and "thoracic surgery". The technology enables the visualisation of the patient's body without making an incision. The idea originates from "flight simulators designed to train fighter pilots" which was then adapted to allow for the mapping of the human body. After planning, the surgeon can easiy demonstrate to their patient how they plan to remove their tumor by handing over the headset and explaining their plan in a 3D environment.

Training @VisualiseVRHealthcare

The link to the article can be found here. AR allows for the simulation of the body in a way which wasn't possible before.
